Forecasting Models Comparison

Model Precision Speed (Training & Forecast) Accuracy Notes
ARIMA Moderate to good for linear trends Fast training, fast forecast Good for linear, stationary data Baseline classical time series model; interpretable
SARIMA Better on seasonal patterns Moderate training, fast forecast Improved accuracy over ARIMA with seasonality Captures seasonality; more parameters than ARIMA
LSTM High precision capturing nonlinearities Slower training; moderately fast forecasting Generally more accurate for complex patterns Requires more data and tuning; captures long-term dependencies
HMM Moderate; models hidden regimes Moderate training and forecast speed Good for regime switching but output flat here Captures market states; may produce flat forecasts
Transformer High precision with global attention Moderate training; fast inference State-of-the-art on sequence data Efficient long-range dependency modeling
Ensemble Highest precision by combining models Depends on models combined (slower overall) More robust, reduces single-model bias Balances strengths; best for production use
